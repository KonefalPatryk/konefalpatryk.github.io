Amarousis Group Project (Unity)

I was a part of a 6 week group project and made an Echo Location game with an Enemy AI that tracks you down through sound.

Collaborated in a 6-week group project to develop an Echo Location game featuring an Enemy AI that tracks the player through sound. Took responsibility for the audio implementation, integrating dynamic sound elements into the gameplay to enhance immersion. Implemented various audio triggers and systems, including: Monster Sounds: Roars (animation trigger), growls (distance checker), attacks (animation trigger), and footsteps (animation trigger). Player Sounds: Breathing (listening to sonar system), slow walking, walking, running, and stomping (listening to sonar system). UI Sounds: Master volume slider, volume mixer, and audio feedback for interactions such as collision breaking, picking up collectables, and skill check results (generator fixes, failure, and success). Map Sounds: Decontamination sprinklers (triggered by alarms), door opening (proximity trigger), and various timers and announcers to create an interactive atmosphere.

Motion Capture Test (Personal Pandemic Project 2020)

I wanted to learn how games work and played around with game files with various programs and learned how to connect a Kinect v1 & Kinect v2 to the computer and how to use it for motion capture and photogrammetry, and then used a character model from the game BattleForge (2009) now called Skylords Reborn, to then use it to learn about rigging and weight painting and combining it all together for a short video.

The recording demonstrates the use of a single Kinect v2 sensor, resulting in noticeable jitter in the animation due to its limitation of tracking from only one direction.

Upon reflection I have learnt how to better optimize an animation through changing the recording environment and making sure it's set under the a ppropriate conditions for the best tracking motion tracking quality. ​Key things I've Learnt: Rigging Weight Painting Motion Capture Photogrammetry ​
